College Ave Picnic

These are the picnic tables on College Avenue from outside Squires Student Center.

Torgersen Bridge

Iconic Torg Bridge, finished in 2000, is a quiet study area for students. It also connects Torgersen Hall to the Newman Library.

The Pylons

The Pylons stand for the eight core values of Virginia Tech: Brotherhood, Honor, Leadership, Sacrifice, Service, Loyalty, Duty and Ut Prosim.

Burruss Hall

Built in 1936, Burruss is the main administration building on Tech's campus. It also has a 3,003 seat auditorium that can hold concerts, art shows and even commencement.

The Drillfield

The Drillfield serves as the barrier between academic buildings and the dorms. It's been an area for cadets to drill since 1894.

Walking to Class

This walkway outside Johnston Student Center is behind Burruss and Pamplin Hall and you can follow it to Turner Place at Lavery Hall.

Walking to Class II

Right outside the front of Pamplin Hall, this walkway connects Davidson Hall, through the Burruss Tunnel, to McBryde Hall.

Holtzman Alumni Center

Built in 2005, the Holtzman Alumni Center hosts conferences, weddings and alumni events.

Walking to Class III

This walkway brings students to Upper Quad, home of many cadet dorms and Shanks Hall.

Upper Quad

Shot right outside newly built Pearson Hall, this photo shows the front of Upper Quad with the American flag in the middle. This is a scene cadets see everyday.

About me

Welcome to Gobbler Gallery! This gallery is made up of photos that depict scenes that students of Virginia Tech see almost every day. If you want to check out what it would be like to walk around the campus in Blacksburg, you've come to the right place! My name is Sean Torney and I am a junior at Virginia Tech studying multimedia journalism. I hope you enjoy looking at my gallery and if you do, you can follow me on my social media platforms below!
